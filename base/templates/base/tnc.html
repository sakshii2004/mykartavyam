1. Definitions

  • Web App: The online platform provided by SJSA for concerned citizens to report issues related to their locality, neighbourhood or any place within India.
  • Citizen: A registered user of the Web App who reports issues pertaining to local problems affecting daily quality of life of an individual/group of people/citizenry at large.
  • SJSA: The abbreviation for the organization Sare Jahan Se Acha Socio Eco Pol Foundation Pvt Ltd, the foundation which is responsible for the development, maintenance, and operation of the Web App.
  • X (formerly Twitter): The third-party social media platform where issues reported through the Web App are automatically posted, subject to X’s own terms and conditions.
  • User Profile: The personal profile created by a Citizen, which includes details such as name, mobile phone number, and optionally, email ID.

2. Registration and Account Details

2.1 Registration Requirements

To use the Web App, you must:

  • Register using a valid mobile phone number.
  • Verify your mobile phone number through an OTP (One-Time Password) sent by government-approved OTP providers.
  • Provide your name and email ID.

Once registered, you can access and use the features of the Web App by reporting issues, uploading images on the platform.

2.2 Account Security

You are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of your account and your login credentials. You must notify SJSA immediately if you believe your account has been compromised or accessed without your authorization.

3. Use of the App

3.1 Reporting Issues

After registration, you can:

  • Upload an image of the issue you are facing.
  • Select the relevant category for the issue from a dropdown list.
  • Provide a landmark location (up to 50 characters).
  • Provide a brief description (up to 300 characters) of the issue.

3.2 Location Tracking

To submit an issue, your phone’s location services must be enabled. The Web App will capture location details automatically when you upload an image. If location services are disabled, you will be prompted to enable them. Provision to enter the nearest landmark (in text format) is available.

3.3 Image Requirements

  • Only still images (photos) are allowed for uploading with a maximum size of 5MB.
  • The image will be checked for correctness by the inbuilt AI/ML process to match the category selected.
  • If the image is objectionable or disturbing, SJSA reserves the right to reject the upload.
  • If there is a mismatch between the image and selected category, SJSA may reject the issue.

3.4 Posting to X

Once an issue is successfully uploaded and validated, it will be posted to X (formerly Twitter), automatically with relevant hashtags, location details, and a unique watermarked number. The posts will be made from the My Kartavyam account and will not display your personal information, such as your phone number or email ID.

4. User Privacy and Data

4.1 User Profile Information

Your personal information, including name, phone number, and email ID, will only be accessible to you through your user profile. Your phone number and name cannot be modified after registration.

4.2 Data Collection

The Web App collects your phone number for registration and authentication. Location data is also captured for each issue you report, and your activity within the Web App (such as issues posted and their status) will be stored and visible to you in your profile.

4.3 Privacy on X

All posts made to X from the Web App will not reveal your personal information. Your identity as the originator of the complaint will remain anonymous to the public on X unless you have logged in to X and respond to the thread.

4.4 Compliance with Terms of X

Any posts made to X are subject to X’s terms and conditions, including their privacy policy, data storage, and purging practices. SJSA is not responsible for how X handles or stores the data associated with your reported issue.

5. Post Status

5.1 Issue Status

  • Once posted, issues remain in an open status for a predetermined number of days.
  • If an issue is unresolved within the specified number of days, it will automatically move to "auto closed" status.
  • If an issue has been auto closed, the User Citizen can reopen it by logging into the Web App using their registered mobile number.

5.2 Disputed/Resolved Issue

SJSA, at its discretion, may mark an issue as “Force Close” based on the feedback or comments from X. This status change will be communicated within the Web App, and the user will be notified accordingly.

Only the User Citizen can mark an issue as Resolved created by them.

5.3 User activity and recognition

Your activity on the Web App, including the number of issues reported and their status, will be tracked. Based on your participation, you may be awarded virtual badges or recognition within the Web App.

6.3 Deletion of Content
